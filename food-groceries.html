Frequently Asked Questions About Grocery Shopping in Abu Dhabi

What's the cheapest supermarket in Abu Dhabi?

Union Coop and Aswaaq are the most budget-friendly supermarkets in Abu Dhabi, with average shopping bills 30-40% lower than premium stores. For even better prices, visit Mina Fruit & Vegetable Market for fresh produce at wholesale rates.

Which supermarkets are open 24 hours in Abu Dhabi?

Choithrams (Al Khalidiya & Corniche branches), Abela Supermarket (Al Khalidiya), and Al Madina Hypermarket (MBZ City) are open 24/7 for emergency shopping needs.

Where can I find Filipino/Turkish/African groceries in Abu Dhabi?

Ethnic specialty stores are concentrated in central Abu Dhabi, especially Electra Street and Tourist Club Area. Popular options include Istanbul Supermarket (Turkish), Filipino Asian Mart, Sudanese African Market, Ethiopian & Eritrean groceries, and West African stores - all in central locations.

Do supermarkets in Abu Dhabi deliver?

Yes, most major supermarkets offer delivery. Lulu, Carrefour, and Choithrams have their own delivery services. You can also order through apps like Instashop, Talabat, and Noon for delivery from multiple stores.

What's the average cost of weekly groceries in Abu Dhabi?

For a family of 4, expect to spend AED 600-1,000 per week at mid-range supermarkets, or AED 400-600 at budget stores. Shopping at Mina Market and cooking at home can reduce costs to AED 350-500 weekly.
