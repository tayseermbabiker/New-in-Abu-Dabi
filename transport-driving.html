Public Transport System

Hafilat Card System

View on Google Maps
  • Where to get: Bus stations, Metro stations, Spinneys, Union Co-op
  • Cost: AED 25 (AED 19 balance + AED 6 card fee)
  • Top-up: Online, machines, or at counters
  • Zones: City (AED 2), Suburb (AED 4), Intercity (AED 25)
  • Validity: 3 years from last use

Main Bus Routes

  • Route 54: Airport → Marina Mall → ADNOC HQ
  • Route 5: Al Wahda Mall → Corniche → Marina Mall
  • Route 32: Khalifa City → Abu Dhabi Mall
  • Route X90: Abu Dhabi → Dubai (Express)
  • Frequency: 10-15 min peak, 20-30 min off-peak

Darb App (Real-time Tracking)

  • Download: App Store / Google Play
  • Features: Live bus locations, route planning, arrival times
  • Journey planner: Enter start/end for best route
  • Hafilat integration: Check balance and top-up

Driving License Conversion

Eligibility by Country

GCC Countries — No Test Required

  • Saudi Arabia, Kuwait, Qatar, Oman, Bahrain
  • Process: Direct exchange same day
  • Cost: AED 200 + admin fees

Direct Conversion Countries

  • USA, Canada, UK, Ireland, Australia, New Zealand
  • EU: Germany, France, Italy, Netherlands, Belgium, etc.
  • Asia: Japan, South Korea, Singapore
  • Requirements: Eye test + document verification only

Test Required Countries

  • India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, Philippines, Egypt, etc.
  • Process: Theory test + road test required
  • Option: Join driving school (recommended)

Step-by-Step Conversion Process

Step 1: Document Preparation

  • Original driving license (valid min 6 months)
  • Certified translation (if not English/Arabic)
  • Passport + UAE residence visa
  • Emirates ID
  • NOC from sponsor/employer

Step 2: Eye Test

  • Centers: Al Mafraq Hospital, Sheikh Khalifa Medical City
  • Cost: AED 50-100
  • Time: 15-30 minutes — Validity: 3 months

Step 3: Application at Traffic HQ

  • Location: Abu Dhabi Police Traffic HQ (Muroor Road)
  • Hours: 7:30 AM - 3:30 PM (Sun-Thu)
  • Processing: Same day for direct conversion
  • Tip: Arrive early to avoid queues

Frequently Asked Questions

How much does it cost to convert a driving license in Abu Dhabi?

Direct conversion (USA, Canada, UK, EU, GCC): AED 690-770 total. Breakdown: Application AED 120, license issuance AED 200, traffic file AED 320, eye test AED 50-100, translation AED 100-150 if needed. With tests required (India, Pakistan, Philippines): AED 970-1,050 including theory test AED 80 and road test AED 200.

How does the Hafilat public transport card work in Abu Dhabi?

Hafilat card costs AED 25 (AED 19 balance + AED 6 card fee). Buy at bus stations, Spinneys, or Union Co-op. Fares: City AED 2, Suburb AED 4, Intercity AED 25. Top-up online or at machines. Card valid 3 years from last use. Use the Darb app to track buses in real-time and plan routes.

Can I convert my driving license from India, Pakistan or Philippines in Abu Dhabi?

Not directly. Licenses from India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, Philippines, and Egypt require both theory and road tests. You can join a driving school (AED 1,800-4,200 for full packages, 4-8 weeks). Emirates Driving Institute and Al Habtoor Driving Center offer fast-track courses with female instructors available.

How much does a taxi or Careem ride cost in Abu Dhabi?

Official TransAD taxis start at AED 12 (day) or AED 15 (night), plus AED 2.30/km within the city. Most city trips cost AED 15-40. Airport rides run AED 70-120. Careem and Uber offer similar pricing with budget and premium options. Call TransAD at +971 600 535 353 for pickup.

Is it better to buy a car or use public transport in Abu Dhabi?

Public transport + taxi costs AED 2,400-3,600 for 6 months (cheapest). A used car costs AED 8,300-9,500 for 6 months including purchase. Summer months (June-August): car is almost necessary due to extreme heat. Families and those working in industrial areas benefit most from owning a car. First 3 months strategy: use public transport to learn the city, then decide.
