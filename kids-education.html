Nursery Rules & Enrolment in Abu Dhabi (ADEK)

Nurseries in Abu Dhabi are licensed and inspected by ADEK (the Department of Education and Knowledge). Here is what every parent should know before enrolling — verified against the official ADEK site (adek.gov.ae).

  • Age: nurseries take children from 45 days up to 4 years old. From age 4, children move on to school (KG/FS1).
  • Licensing: every nursery must be ADEK-licensed, use an established curriculum, keep a medical record for each child, and make its policies available to parents.
  • Fees: nursery fees must be approved by ADEK. Nurseries file any fee changes with ADEK between January and April, and approved changes apply from September — not a parent registration deadline.
  • Enrolment: you register directly at the nursery, anytime, per its own admission policy. Typical documents: the child's and parents' Emirates ID, the child's birth certificate, passport & residence visa copies, and up-to-date vaccination records; some nurseries also ask for a tenancy contract (Tawtheeq).

Always confirm current rules and fees on the official ADEK site (adek.gov.ae) and with the nursery directly. Last verified: July 2026.

Fee Tiers & Budget Planning

How Nursery Fees Work (Read Before Budgeting)

  • Each nursery sets its own fees, and none on this list publishes a standard public fee schedule — always confirm the current fee directly with the nursery.
  • Fees vary by curriculum, number of days per week, age group and area.
  • Under ADEK rules, nurseries file any fee changes with the Department between January and April each year, applied from September (adek.gov.ae).
  • Rough 2026 market guide only (from directory listings, not official): budget nurseries from about AED 15,000/year, mid-range roughly AED 30,000-45,000, premium/international AED 50,000-65,000+.

Enrollment Guide & Tips

Best Time to Apply

  • February-April: Peak admission season for September start
  • Wait lists are common - apply to 2-3 nurseries simultaneously
  • Apply 6 months before your desired start date
  • Visit during operational hours to see facilities and meet teachers
  • Most nurseries offer tours on weekdays 9 AM - 12 PM

Required Documents

  • Passport copies (parents and child)
  • UAE residence visa
  • Emirates ID
  • Vaccination records (up to date)
  • Birth certificate (translated if not in English/Arabic)

Choosing the Right Nursery

  • British EYFS: Best for families planning UK/British school pathway
  • Montessori: Best for encouraging independence and self-directed learning
  • Reggio Emilia: Best for creative, project-based learning
  • Islamic curriculum: Available at Noha's Ark with EYFS standards
  • Location matters: Consider proximity to home and work

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the best preschool in Abu Dhabi?

Well-regarded Abu Dhabi nurseries include Redwood Montessori (multilingual English, Arabic and French, with branches across the city), Discovery Nursery (British Foundation Stage, Al Bateen), Falcon British Nursery (UK EYFS, trilingual, Al Rawdah near the Grand Mosque), British Orchard and Odyssey. Greenopia offers a nature-based Reggio Emilia approach. Curricula range from British EYFS to Montessori and Reggio. Fees are not standardised, so confirm the current fee directly with each nursery.

How much does nursery cost in Abu Dhabi?

Nursery fees in Abu Dhabi are not standardised and no nursery publishes a fixed public rate — they vary by curriculum, days per week, age and area. As a rough 2026 market guide (directory listings, not official), budget nurseries start around AED 15,000/year, mid-range roughly AED 30,000-45,000, and premium or international AED 50,000-65,000+. Under ADEK rules nurseries file fee changes between January and April, applied from September. Budget separately for registration, uniform and materials, and always confirm the current fee directly with the nursery.

When should I apply for nursery in Abu Dhabi?

Best time: February-April for September start (peak admission season). Popular nurseries fill up quickly - apply 6 months before your desired start date. Wait lists are common, so apply to 2-3 nurseries. Visit during operational hours to see facilities, meet teachers, and observe classes. Most nurseries offer tours on weekdays 9 AM-12 PM. Bring required documents: passport copies, visa, Emirates ID, vaccination records.

What is the difference between British and Montessori nurseries?

British nurseries (Discovery, Falcon): Follow Early Years Foundation Stage (EYFS) curriculum, structured play-based learning, prepare for British primary schools. Montessori nurseries (Redwood): Child-led learning, hands-on activities, mixed age groups, focus on independence and self-directed exploration. British better for: Families planning UK/British school pathway, structured routines. Montessori better for: Encouraging independence, multilingual education, child-paced learning.

Are there affordable nurseries in Abu Dhabi under AED 40,000?

Yes — more affordable, ADEK-licensed nurseries exist, typically in areas such as Mussafah, Mohammed bin Zayed City, Al Shamkha and Khalifa City rather than the city centre. Trade-offs can include fewer premium facilities and larger class sizes. Because fees are not published in a standard schedule, contact each nursery directly for its current budget or part-time rates.
