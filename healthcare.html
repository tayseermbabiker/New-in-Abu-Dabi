Frequently Asked Questions About Healthcare in Abu Dhabi

What's the cheapest hospital without insurance in Abu Dhabi?

Sheikh Khalifa Medical City and Al Mafraq Hospital are the most affordable, with general consultations at AED 50-100 and emergency care often free for UAE residents. For non-residents, pharmacy clinics (AED 50-75) are the cheapest option for minor health issues.

Can I go to the hospital without health insurance in Abu Dhabi?

Yes, you can visit any hospital without insurance, but you'll pay out-of-pocket. Public hospitals are most affordable (AED 50-200 per visit). Private hospitals can charge AED 300-500 for consultations. Health insurance is mandatory for all UAE residents, so check with your employer.

Where can I get cheap medical consultation in Abu Dhabi?

Cheapest options: 1) Pharmacy clinics at Life Pharmacy or Boots (AED 50-75), 2) Public hospitals like SKMC (AED 50-100), 3) Walk-in clinics during weekday mornings for lower rates. Avoid private hospital emergency rooms unless necessary as they charge AED 300-500.

What is FAZAA card and how does it help with healthcare costs?

FAZAA is a discount card for UAE residents offering 50% off at participating hospitals and clinics. Available for government employees, their families, and some private sector workers. Check fazaa.ae for participating healthcare providers and application process.

How much does emergency care cost in Abu Dhabi?

Emergency care costs: Public hospitals (Sheikh Khalifa, Al Mafraq) - Free to AED 50 for UAE residents. Private hospitals - AED 300-500 for initial consultation, plus additional costs for tests/treatment. Always go to public hospitals for true emergencies to avoid high costs.
